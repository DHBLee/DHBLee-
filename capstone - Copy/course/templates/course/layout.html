About my Course

This course will teach learners how to properly play a song with these three valuable info:

  • Dynamics
  • Rhythm
  • Fluidity

The course provides three main sections:

Strumming
  • Strumming is often used to play chords, making it essential for accompanying vocals or other instruments.
  • Different strumming patterns can bring out the unique characteristics of each chord progression, adding texture and dynamics to the music.
  • Varying your strumming technique (speed, force, and dynamics) allows for greater expressiveness in your playing.

* here is an example video

Plucking
  • Plucking involves using the fingers or a pick to individually strike or pull the strings of the guitar, producing sound.
  • Plucking can create different tonal qualities and dynamics compared to strumming. The way you pluck a string (the force and angle) can significantly affect the sound produced.
  • It allows for expressive playing, enabling musicians to convey emotions and nuances in their performance.

* here is an example video

Fingerstyle
  • Fingerstyle is a popular and versatile technique used in acoustic guitar playing that usually involves plucking the strings with the fingers rather than using a pick (other than hybrid picking).
  • Its especially effective for playing intricate arrangements, where the guitar takes on multiple roles (melody, harmony, rhythm).
  • There are many variation fingerstyle techniques, this includes: Basic Fingerpicking, Arpeggios, Travis Picking, Harmonics, Hybrid Picking, and many more.

FAQs

How much is your course? ▼
0$! This course is free to use.
After this course, what should go next? ▼
After understunding the fundamentlas of strumming, plucking, and fingerstyle, it will be easier to learn a song through videos, tabs, or by listening to the song!
What level of playing is required as a prerequisite for this course? ▼
Ideal knowledge would be knowing some simple chords and simple strumming patterns. From there, this course will guide you to enhance your guitar playing skills!
What if I only have an electric guitar, will this course still be relevant? ▼
To some extent. In an acoustic guitar, you'll have more control in your dynamics and force, so you'll be able to implement more of the learnings here in this course. But nonetheless, the course still has information that you can do in an electric guitar. Also, this course is free.
Will this course tackle heavily on thoery, scales, and exercises? ▼
Not at all! This course focuses on how we translate a song to our guitar while retaining the song's characteristics via Strumming/Plucking/Fingerstyle.
I am already familiar with these three techniques. Do I still need to take this course? ▼
These three techniques (Strumming, Plucking, and Fingerstyle) has its own skill level. For me, having this technique that produces a fluidity, seamless, guitar sound that matches the song's characteristics is the ideal. So if you think your playing style is still stiff, then this course will help you conquer that!
